# Agentic-Causal-World-Models-ACWM-Toward-Self-Correcting-Autonomous-Intelligence

The rapid advancement of large language models and autonomous agent frameworks has accelerated the development of intelligent systems capable of 
performing complex tasks involving planning, reasoning, memory utilization, tool interaction, and 
iterative decision-making architectures. Recent agentic have demonstrated remarkable competence in executing multi-step objectives 
across diverse domains. Nevertheless, despite these achievements, most existing agents continue to rely 
predominantly on correlational reasoning, deriving decisions from statistical associations 
learned during training or accumulated through experience. Therefore, they often struggle to 
identify the underlying causes of observed outcomes, evaluate the consequences of unchosen 
alternatives, or adapt their future behavior based on explicit causal explanations. This limitation 
presents a substantial obstacle to the realization of robust and genuinely autonomous intelligence.
